## APPETITE DRIVER — 18 Questions

### D1.1: Stimulation-Seeking (4)
- A1: I feel most alive when experiencing something new, challenging, or intense.
- A2: I take on new projects or initiatives with excitement but sometimes move to the next thing before finishing.
- A3: I'm drawn to experiences and challenges that are edgy or push my boundaries.
- A4: When my work or life becomes too routine or predictable, I feel restless.

### D1.2: Hedonic/Comfort-Seeking (4)
- A5: I find comfort and satisfaction in familiar routines, established traditions, or things I know well.
- A6: When stressed by work or family demands, I reach for food, entertainment, or other pleasures.
- A7: I sometimes overindulge (eating, drinking, shopping, screen time) even when I know I should moderate.
- A8: I'm intentional about how I spend resources on comfort and leisure activities. [REVERSE]

### D1.3: Gratification Timing (2)
- A9: When I get genuinely excited about an opportunity, I dive in with full commitment.
- A10: I'm more energized by periods of high intensity and challenge than by sustained, steady-paced work.

### D1.4: Boredom Relationship (2)
- A12: Boredom is one of the hardest states for me to tolerate.
- A13: Extended periods of routine or predictability wear on my energy over time.

### D1.5: Sensory/Aesthetic (1)
- A15: I notice beauty, aesthetics, and sensory details more than most people around me.

### D1.6: Risk Tolerance (1)
- A18: I'm drawn toward professional or personal pursuits that carry an element of risk or uncertainty.

### D1.7: Humor/Levity (4)
- A21: I see humor in difficult situations and use it to navigate challenges.
- A22: I can laugh and find levity in hard things without making others feel dismissed.
- A23: People would say I'm naturally lighthearted, fun, and bring levity to work or social situations.
- A24: Humor is one of my tools for processing difficult things without avoiding them.

## APPROVAL DRIVER — 18 Questions

### D2.1: Public/Social Validation (4)
- B1: I'm aware of how I'm perceived in professional and social circles and it affects my presentation.
- B2: I feel a genuine lift when I receive recognition from colleagues, peers, or respected community members.
- B3: When I accomplish something significant, I want people in my circles to know about it.
- B4: My sense of how I'm doing is meaningfully affected by feedback from people I respect.

### D2.2: Peer/Professional Validation (2)
- B5: I want acknowledgment from colleagues and peers when I've contributed something meaningful.
- B6: When my work or leadership goes unrecognized by people I respect, it affects me.

### D2.3: Relational/Intimate Validation (2)
- B8: When someone close to me seems disappointed in a choice or action I've taken, I replay it looking for what I missed.
- B11: I sometimes say yes to people I care about even when the honest answer should be no.

### D2.4: Comparison Orientation (2)
- B12: I measure my career and life progress against specific peers or people at my life stage.
- B13: When someone in my network or field succeeds, I notice my internal response before I notice genuine happiness for them.

### D2.5: Conflict Avoidance vs. Relational Courage (2)
- B15: I tell people what I honestly see even if it might create friction or risk the relationship.
- B16: I speak up in professional or personal settings even when it creates tension with people who matter.

### D2.6: Belonging/Community (2)
- B18: I feel genuinely energized by being part of a team, organization, or larger community.
- B19: I invest significantly in the health and cohesion of groups or organizations I'm part of.

### D2.7: Rejection Sensitivity (2)
- B21: When I feel rejected or excluded by someone professionally or personally important, it affects my mood and functioning.
- B22: When I sense a valued colleague or person pulling away, I work to understand and re-establish connection.

## AMBITION DRIVER — 26 Questions

### D3.1: Achievement Orientation (3)
- C1: I feel a pull toward achievement and productivity even during vacations or periods meant for rest.
- C2: I take genuine satisfaction in completing significant projects and reaching major milestones.
- C3: When I'm not making progress toward something that matters, I feel dissatisfied and internally restless.

### D3.2: Impact/Legacy (4)
- C4: I think regularly about what I'm building or creating that will have impact years from now.
- C5: I'm motivated to invest in people and initiatives that will have significance beyond my direct involvement.
- C6: I measure my career's significance by its lasting impact and influence, not just immediate results.
- C7: I'm drawn to challenges large enough to matter at a scope beyond my immediate organization or circle.

### D3.3: Recognition/Status — Scenarios (4)
- **C8:** You lead a major initiative to success. The organization celebrates the team, but your specific contribution isn't highlighted. Your honest feeling:
  - a. The initiative succeeded and the team was recognized — that's what matters.
  - b. Mild wistfulness about not being named, but it passes.
  - c. Internal frustration about the credit distribution that I manage.
  - d. Frustration that affects how I experience the success.

- **C9:** You invested significantly in developing a leader. They achieve prominence without acknowledging your role. Your response:
  - a. Genuine pride — that was exactly what the investment was for.
  - b. Mild disappointment that moves past quickly.
  - c. A sense of loss I carry that I think is disproportionate.
  - d. Significant hurt — feels like they didn't acknowledge what mattered.

- **C10:** You led a significant initiative that fails publicly. You were responsible for key decisions. Afterward:
  - a. I own it clearly, transparently, and move forward on lessons learned.
  - b. I own it publicly but privately revisit what others could have done differently.
  - c. I own my decisions but find the public nature of the failure difficult.
  - d. I struggle to separate my assessment of the failure from my assessment of myself.

- **C11:** Someone less qualified is placed in leadership above you. You're convinced it's the wrong choice. Over the months:
  - a. I commit to making them successful — the organization's success matters most.
  - b. I do my job well while privately holding my reservations about the decision.
  - c. My engagement and motivation are genuinely affected, though I manage the behavior.
  - d. I look seriously for an exit — I can't sustain full contribution in this structure.

### D3.4: Competitive Orientation (3)
- C12: I'm aware of how my career and accomplishments compare to peers at my stage.
- C13: The successes of people in my field or organization motivate me to raise my own performance.
- C14: I'm energized by professional environments where performance is evaluated or ranked.

### D3.5: Leadership/Influence (3)
- C15: I feel most effective and most myself when I'm setting direction for a team or organization.
- C16: I'm drawn to roles where significant outcomes depend on my ability to influence and lead people.
- C17: It's difficult for me to fully engage without being in a position to shape strategy or direction.

### D3.6: Mastery/Excellence (3)
- C18: I find it hard to release work I know could be better, even when further improvement won't be seen.
- C19: I'd rather develop true mastery and depth in key areas than be generally competent across many.
- C20: I hold myself and my work to standards that most people would consider unnecessarily rigorous.
