## APPETITE DRIVER — 18 Questions

### D1.1: Stimulation-Seeking (4)
- A1: I feel most alive when experiencing something new or intense.
- A2: I start new projects or hobbies excited but often abandon them before finishing.
- A3: I'm drawn to experiences that are edgy, adventurous, or push my boundaries.
- A4: When my work or life becomes too predictable, I feel restless.

### D1.2: Hedonic/Comfort-Seeking (4)
- A5: I find comfort and satisfaction in familiar routines and established patterns.
- A6: When stressed by work or relationships, I reach for food, entertainment, or other pleasures.
- A7: I sometimes overspend or overindulge (eating, drinking, shopping) even when I know I should stop.
- A8: I think carefully about how I spend money and time on comfort and leisure. [REVERSE]

### D1.3: Gratification Timing (2)
- A9: I dive fully into new projects or opportunities when I get excited about them.
- A10: I'm more energized by intense short projects than by sustained long-term effort.

### D1.4: Boredom Relationship (2)
- A12: Boredom is one of the hardest things for me to tolerate.
- A13: Routine and predictability in work or life wear me down over time.

### D1.5: Sensory/Aesthetic (1)
- A15: I notice and appreciate beauty, aesthetics, and sensory details more than most people.

### D1.6: Risk Tolerance (1)
- A18: I'm drawn toward career or personal activities that carry some risk or uncertainty.

### D1.7: Humor/Levity (4)
- A21: I see humor in things and use it to navigate work and life challenges.
- A22: I can laugh at difficult situations without making others feel diminished.
- A23: People would say I'm naturally lighthearted and fun to work or socialize with.
- A24: Humor helps me process hard things without avoiding the real issue.

## APPROVAL DRIVER — 18 Questions

### D2.1: Public/Social Validation (4)
- B1: I'm aware of how colleagues and social circles perceive me and it affects how I present myself.
- B2: I feel a genuine lift when I receive recognition from colleagues or people in my field.
- B3: When I accomplish something at work or personally, I want the right people to know about it.
- B4: My sense of how I'm doing in my career is affected by feedback from people I respect.

### D2.2: Peer/Professional Validation (2)
- B5: I want acknowledgment from colleagues when I've contributed something meaningful to a project.
- B6: When my work goes unrecognized by people I respect professionally, it bothers me.

### D2.3: Relational/Intimate Validation (2)
- B8: When someone I'm close to seems disappointed in me, I replay it and look for what I did wrong.
- B11: I say yes to people I care about even when the honest answer should be no.

### D2.4: Comparison Orientation (2)
- B12: I measure my career progress against specific peers I pay attention to.
- B13: When someone in my professional circle succeeds, I notice my internal response before genuine happiness.

### D2.5: Conflict Avoidance vs. Relational Courage (2)
- B15: I tell people what I honestly see even if it might damage the relationship.
- B16: I speak up at work even when it creates friction with people whose opinion matters.

### D2.6: Belonging/Community (2)
- B18: I feel energized by being part of a team, organization, or community.
- B19: I invest in the health and cohesion of teams or groups I belong to, even when it costs me.

### D2.7: Rejection Sensitivity (2)
- B21: When I feel rejected by someone professional or personally important, it affects me for days.
- B22: When I sense a colleague or person I care about pulling away, I work to re-establish connection.

## AMBITION DRIVER — 26 Questions

### D3.1: Achievement Orientation (3)
- C1: I feel a pull toward productivity and advancement even during periods meant for rest or vacation.
- C2: I take genuine satisfaction in completing projects and reaching professional or personal milestones.
- C3: When I'm not making progress toward something meaningful, I feel dissatisfied.

### D3.2: Impact/Legacy (4)
- C4: I think about what I'm building in my career that will still matter years from now.
- C5: I'm motivated to invest in people and projects that will have impact beyond my direct involvement.
- C6: I measure my career success by lasting effect and influence, not just immediate results.
- C7: I'm drawn to challenges and opportunities significant enough to matter at a scale beyond my immediate work.

### D3.3: Recognition/Status — Scenarios (4)
- **C8:** You led a significant project to success. Your team gets credit; your contribution isn't specifically named. Your honest feeling:
  - a. The project succeeded — that's what matters.
  - b. Mild wistfulness but it passes.
  - c. Frustration I manage internally.
  - d. Frustration that affects how I feel about the success.

- **C9:** You invested significantly in developing a colleague. They go on to success without mentioning your role. Your response:
  - a. Pride — this is exactly what the investment was for.
  - b. Mild disappointment that passes.
  - c. A sense of loss I carry longer than I think is warranted.
  - d. Significant hurt — feels like a failure on their part.

- **C10:** You led a significant initiative that failed publicly. You were clearly responsible. In the weeks after:
  - a. I own it clearly and focus forward on what I learned.
  - b. I own it publicly but privately revisit what others could have done.
  - c. I own my decisions but the public failure is much harder to carry.
  - d. I struggle to separate the failure from my own competence.

- **C11:** Someone less experienced is promoted into a leadership role above you. You believe it's the wrong choice. Over the following months:
  - a. I commit to making them successful.
  - b. I do my job well while privately holding my assessment.
  - c. My motivation is genuinely affected, though I manage it.
  - d. I look for an exit — I can't sustain full contribution in this structure.

### D3.4: Competitive Orientation (3)
- C12: I track how my career progress compares to peers at similar stages.
- C13: The success of people in my field or company spurs me to push harder.
- C14: I'm energized by work environments where performance is ranked or compared.

### D3.5: Leadership/Influence (3)
- C15: I feel most effective and like myself when I'm setting direction for a team or project.
- C16: I'm drawn to roles where outcomes depend significantly on my ability to influence people.
- C17: It's difficult for me to contribute fully without being in a position to shape direction.

### D3.6: Mastery/Excellence (3)
- C18: I struggle to release work I know could be better, even if further improvement won't be visible.
- C19: I'd rather develop genuine expertise in a few areas than be competent across many.
- C20: I hold myself to a professional standard that most people would consider unnecessarily high.
